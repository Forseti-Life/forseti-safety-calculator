# Individual Metrics - Nested Exploratory Tree Structure

This document organizes metrics into a conditional hierarchy where answers to primary questions determine which follow-up questions are asked.

---

## SAFE DIMENSION - Security & Protection

### 1. Police / Law Enforcement
**PRIMARY SCREENING:**
- **Police contact past year** - Had any police contact (victim/witness/accused): yes/no

**IF YES → Ask:**
  - **Contact frequency** - Number of police contacts (past year)
  - **Contact type** - Victim/witness/accused/other
  - **Crime victimization** - Victim of crime: yes/no
    - **IF VICTIM → Ask:**
      - **Crime victimization count** - Number of times victim (past 5 years)
      - **Restraining orders held** - Number of active protection orders
      - **Crime reporting rate** - Reported to police: always/sometimes/never

**IF NO → Ask:**
- **General safety perception** - Feel safe from crime (scale 0-10)
- **Personal security spending** - Annual cost for security measures ($)
- **Neighborhood watch participation** - Active member: yes/no

---

### 2. Fire Safety
**PRIMARY SCREENING:**
- **Fire safety preparedness** - Home has basic fire safety: yes/no

**IF YES → Ask:**
  - **Smoke detector functionality** - Working detectors/total needed (ratio)
  - **Fire extinguisher access** - Number in home/vehicles
  - **Fire escape plan practiced** - Practice evacuation: never/annually/quarterly

**IF NO → Ask:**
  - **Fire safety barriers** - Why no fire safety (cost/knowledge/landlord)
  - **Fire insurance coverage** - Property insured for fire: yes/no
  - **Fire risk factors** - Known fire hazards in home (number)

**ALL ASK:**
- **Fire incident history** - House fires experienced (lifetime)

---

### 3. Emergency Medical Response
**PRIMARY SCREENING:**
- **Called ambulance ever** - Used EMS in past 5 years: yes/no

**IF YES → Ask:**
  - **EMS call frequency** - Times called ambulance (past 5 years)
  - **EMS response satisfaction** - Response time acceptable (scale 0-10)
  - **Ambulance cost burden** - Could afford ambulance: yes/no
    - **IF NO → Ask:**
      - **Medical transport alternatives** - Other ways to get emergency care

**IF NO → Ask:**
- **First aid capability** - Can provide basic first aid: yes/no
- **First aid certification** - Current certification: yes/no
- **Medical emergency kit** - Have first aid kit: yes/no

**ALL ASK:**
- **Health insurance ambulance coverage** - EMS covered: yes/full/partial/no

---

### 4. Emergency Preparedness
**PRIMARY SCREENING:**
- **Emergency preparedness level** - Prepared for disaster: well/somewhat/not at all

**IF WELL/SOMEWHAT → Ask:**
  - **Days of emergency supplies** - Food, water, meds on hand (days)
  - **Evacuation plan documented** - Written evacuation plan: yes/no
  - **Emergency spending** - Annual cost for preparedness ($)

**IF NOT AT ALL → Ask:**
  - **Preparedness barriers** - Why not prepared (cost/knowledge/time)
  - **Emergency awareness** - Know local disaster risks: yes/no

**ALL ASK:**
- **Emergency contact network** - People who can help in crisis (number)
- **Property disaster insurance** - Flood/earthquake coverage: yes/no

---

### 5. Mental Health Crisis Support
**PRIMARY SCREENING:**
- **Mental health crisis history** - Mental health crisis (past 5 years): yes/no

**IF YES → Ask:**
  - **Crisis service usage** - Used crisis services: yes/no
    - **IF YES → Ask:**
      - **Crisis service effectiveness** - Services helpful (scale 0-10)
      - **Crisis hospitalization** - Psychiatric hospitalizations (number)
  - **Crisis support network** - People to call in crisis (number)

**IF NO → Ask:**
- **Mental health awareness** - Know how to get crisis help: yes/no
- **Mental Health First Aid** - Completed MHFA training: yes/no

**ALL ASK:**
- **Mental health crisis plan** - Written crisis plan: yes/no
- **Crisis hotline saved** - 988 or crisis line accessible: yes/no

## ENERGIZED DIMENSION - Basic Needs & Resources

### 6. Housing Situation
**PRIMARY SCREENING:**
- **Housing stability** - Housing status: own/rent-stable/rent-unstable/homeless/temporary

**IF OWN → Ask:**
  - **Home maintenance fund** - Dollars saved for repairs
  - **Home age/condition** - Years since major updates
  - **Property insurance** - Homeowner's insurance: yes/no

**IF RENT-STABLE → Ask:**
  - **Rent burden** - Percentage of income on rent
  - **Lease security** - Lease term: year/month-to-month
  - **Renter's insurance** - Coverage: yes/no

**IF RENT-UNSTABLE → Ask:**
  - **Eviction risk** - Risk of eviction: high/medium/low
  - **Rent arrears** - Months behind on rent
  - **Housing assistance** - Receiving Section 8/voucher: yes/no

**IF HOMELESS/TEMPORARY → Ask:**
  - **Shelter access** - Using shelter: yes/no
  - **Homeless duration** - Months without stable housing
  - **Housing search** - Actively seeking housing: yes/no

**ALL ASK:**
- **Housing quality issues** - Unresolved maintenance issues (number)
- **Overcrowding** - People per room ratio

---

### 7. Food Security
**PRIMARY SCREENING:**
- **Food security status** - Food situation: secure/marginal/low/very low

**IF SECURE → Ask:**
  - **Food quality** - Fresh produce servings per day
  - **Food budget flexibility** - Can afford preferred foods (scale 0-10)
  - **Dietary needs met** - Special diets accessible: yes/no

**IF MARGINAL/LOW/VERY LOW → Ask:**
  - **Food assistance usage** - Use SNAP/WIC/food bank: yes/no
    - **IF YES → Ask:**
      - **SNAP benefit amount** - Monthly food assistance ($)
      - **Food bank frequency** - Times per month use food pantry
  - **Meal skipping** - Skip meals due to cost: never/rarely/sometimes/often
  - **Children's meals** - Kids get 3 meals daily: always/sometimes/never
  - **Food affordability** - Percentage of income on food

**ALL ASK:**
- **Days of food stored** - Days could eat without shopping
- **Distance to grocery store** - Miles to supermarket

---

### 8. Employment & Income
**PRIMARY SCREENING:**
- **Employment status** - Current: employed-FT/employed-PT/unemployed/retired/disabled

**IF EMPLOYED → Ask:**
  - **Income level** - Annual gross income ($)
    - **IF < $50K → Ask:**
      - **Living wage gap** - Income vs living wage (%)
      - **Side income** - Additional income sources (number)
  - **Job stability** - Job security perception (scale 0-10)
  - **Income variability** - Income varies month-to-month: yes/no
  - **Benefits access** - Employer health insurance: yes/no

**IF UNEMPLOYED → Ask:**
  - **Unemployment duration** - Months without work
  - **Unemployment benefits** - Receiving unemployment: yes/no
  - **Job search status** - Actively seeking work: yes/no
  - **Unemployment reason** - Laid off/quit/other

**IF RETIRED → Ask:**
  - **Retirement income sources** - Number of income streams
  - **Pension/SS adequacy** - Income meets needs (scale 0-10)

**IF DISABLED → Ask:**
  - **Disability income** - Receiving SSDI/SSI: yes/no
  - **Disability benefits adequacy** - Benefits meet needs (scale 0-10)

**ALL ASK:**
- **Income consistency** - Reliable income: yes/no

---

### 9. Financial Health
**PRIMARY SCREENING:**
- **Net worth** - Total assets minus debts ($)

**IF NET WORTH > $1M → Ask:**
  - **Investment portfolio** - Diversified investments: yes/no
  - **Retirement savings** - Retirement accounts balance ($)
  - **Financial advisor** - Use financial advisor: yes/no

**IF NET WORTH $250K-$1M → Ask:**
  - **Emergency fund adequacy** - Months of expenses saved
  - **Retirement on track** - Retirement savings adequate (scale 0-10)
  - **Investment strategy** - Actively investing: yes/no

**IF NET WORTH $0-$250K → Ask:**
  - **Liquid savings** - Savings account balance ($)
  - **Emergency fund** - Could handle $400 emergency: yes/no
  - **Debt-to-income ratio** - Monthly debt payments/income (%)

**IF NET WORTH < $0 (NEGATIVE) → Ask:**
  - **Total debt** - All debt excluding mortgage ($)
  - **Medical debt** - Medical bills owed ($)
  - **Student loan debt** - Student loans owed ($)
  - **Credit card debt** - CC balances ($)
  - **Debt management** - In debt repayment plan: yes/no
  - **Bankruptcy consideration** - Considering bankruptcy: yes/no

**ALL ASK:**
- **Financial stress** - Financial stress level (scale 0-10)

---

### 10. Healthcare Access

- **Health insurance status** - Coverage: insured/underinsured/uninsured

**IF INSURED → Ask:**

  - **Primary care doctor** - Have PCP: yes/no
    - **IF YES → Ask:**
      - **Preventive care** - Annual checkup: yes/no

    - **IF NO → Ask:**

**IF UNDERINSURED → Ask:**

  - **Out-of-pocket burden** - Percentage income on healthcare
  - **Delayed care** - Delayed care due to cost (past year): yes/no

**IF UNINSURED → Ask:**
  - **Uninsured duration** - Months without insurance
  - **Healthcare access** - Where get care (ER/clinic/nowhere)
  - **Medicaid eligibility** - Eligible but not enrolled: yes/no

**ALL ASK:**
- **Unmet healthcare needs** - Needed care but didn't get (past year): yes/no

## SUMMARY: Conditional Logic Categories

### **GATEWAY QUESTIONS** (Asked First)
These determine which follow-up paths are taken:
- Net worth level
- Housing status
- Food security status
- Employment status
- Health insurance status
- Police contact history
- Family contact
- Community involvement
- Discrimination experience
- Education level
- Volunteer activity
- Self-rated health
- Substance use

### **SKIP LOGIC EXAMPLES**
1. **High net worth** (>$1M) → Skip debt questions
2. **No police contact** → Skip incident details
3. **Secure housing** → Skip eviction/arrears questions
4. **Food secure** → Skip assistance/meal skipping
5. **Employed FT** → Skip unemployment questions
6. **Insured** → Skip uninsured barriers
7. **No substance concern** → Skip all substance questions
8. **No discrimination** → Skip reporting/resolution questions
9. **Bachelor's+** → Skip GED/basic education
10. **Excellent health** → Skip chronic disease management

### **DEPTH LEVELS**
- **Level 1**: Screening question (everyone answers)
- **Level 2**: Conditional based on L1 response
- **Level 3**: Conditional based on L2 response
- **Universal**: Always asked regardless of path
